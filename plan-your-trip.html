Plan Your Trip

Money, power, safety, and local customs — quick answers to the questions visitors ask most.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Euros and yen are often accepted, and banks on the island can exchange other currencies. Major credit cards are widely accepted.

Power on Taniti is 120 volts — the same as in the United States, so U.S. visitors don't need an adapter.

Alcohol may not be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age is 18, though it is loosely enforced.

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in Taniti City and tourist areas. In rural parts of the island, little English is spoken.

Violent crime on Taniti is rare, but petty crime has been rising — keep an eye on your belongings and watch for pickpockets in busy areas.

Taniti observes many national holidays, and attractions and restaurants often close on those days. Check dates before you travel and plan ahead.

The island has one hospital and several clinics, staffed by multilingual teams.

Taxis serve Taniti City and a rental car agency operates near the airport. See Getting Around for buses, bikes, and arrival details.
